Miniature Satellite Development Workshop

Nov 26-27, 2018 | 9 A.M - 4 P.M

STEMdroid, Bangalore

Description

Start building your own satellite just with a soda can. The program includes complete overview satellite design and it's working, introduction to CANSAT and CubeSat, components and working of rockets, introduction to sounding rockets and balloons, parachute designing, fundamentals of radio communication and Arduino Platform.

A CANSAT is a miniature satellite built using a soda can and open hardware. CANSAT is launched using a sounding balloon and descends back using a parachute.

Opencube Labs is one India’s first Space Technology start-ups and is the winner of NASA International Space challenge. Our team consists of various expertise having achievements and professional certifications from Harvard, MIT, California Institute of Technology, and IBM.

Topics Covered

  • Introduction to Satellite design & it's working
  • Introduction to CanSat & it's history
  • Introduction to CubeSat(1kg Satellite) & other nano-satellites
  • Overview of the CanSat Development Kit
  • Introduction to Arduino Platform
  • Introduction to all the sensors & interfacing
  • Designing the CanSat structure
  • Integrating all CanSat modules
  • Introduction to parachute design
  • Simulation of ground station
  • Fundamentals of Radio communication
  • Overview of sounding rockets & High Altitude Balloons(HABs)

Day Wise Spilt-Up

Hardware kits are provided to participants during the workshop and participants work in teams of 2-3. Kits are taken back post the workshop and are available to be bought at retail price later.

Age limit: 10+ yrs

Takeaways

  • Get opportunity to be part of your own mission to near space
  • Start designing your own satellite payloads
  • Earn an open badge (Open badges are similar to digital certificates)
